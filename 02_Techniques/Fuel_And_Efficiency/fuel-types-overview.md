# Fuel Types Overview

Camp stove fuel choice depends on stove type, temperature, trip style, and availability.

## Propane

Strong cold-weather performance, but usually heavier and better suited to car camping or basecamp systems.

## Butane

Convenient canister design, often more suitable for car camping than backpacking.

## Isobutane

Better performance than standard butane in colder or more demanding conditions.

## Propane/Isobutane Blend

The most common backpacking canister fuel type for JetBoil-style systems. It balances weight, performance, and temperature range.

## White Gas

Useful in extreme cold, but messier and more demanding. Best for experienced users.

## Kerosene

Available and inexpensive, but smoky and sooty.

## Alcohol

Light and simple, but slower and less powerful.

## Wood

Can be practical in some locations, but depends on dry fuel availability and fire restrictions.
