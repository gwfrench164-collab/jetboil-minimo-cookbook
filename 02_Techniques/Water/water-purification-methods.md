# Water Purification Methods

Safe water is essential in the backcountry. JetBoil's article emphasizes that backpackers should ideally carry two purification options so there is a backup if one method fails.

## Boiling

Boiling is simple and reliable. Bring water to a full boil for 1 minute, or 3 minutes at higher elevations.

## Tablets and Crystals

Chemical treatment is light and convenient, but may have limitations with sediment and some organisms.

## Filters

Filters remove many biological hazards, especially protozoa and bacteria, but may not remove viruses, chemicals, or heavy metals unless specifically designed to do so.

## Purifiers

Purifiers are more robust than filters and may remove viruses and other hazards depending on design.

## UV Treatment

UV treatment is light and convenient, but cloudy or sediment-heavy water may need filtering first.

## MiniMo Field Note

Boiling water in the MiniMo is one of the most useful overlap techniques because the same water can be used for drinking, coffee, tea, and just-add-water meals.
