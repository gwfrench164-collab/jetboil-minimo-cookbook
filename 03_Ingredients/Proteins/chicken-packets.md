# Chicken Packets

## Summary

Shelf-stable protein for rice, pasta, wraps, soups, and skillet meals.

## Why Carry It

Chicken Packets is useful because it is shelf-stable, practical for camp cooking, and connects to multiple recipes or meal systems.

## Common Forms

- pouch chicken
- foil chicken packet
- canned chicken alternative

## Best Uses

- backpacking
- car camping
- rice meals
- pasta meals
- wraps

## Related Recipes

- Camp Chicken Fried Rice
- Angel Hair Pasta with Chicken and Artichokes
- Sesame Chicken

## Related Techniques

- Backpacking Protein Systems
- Trail Pantry Essentials
- Just Add Water Meals

## Substitutions

- freeze-dried chicken
- tuna packets
- salmon packets

## Fresh vs Shelf-Stable

- Fresh alternative: cooked chicken
- Shelf-stable alternative: chicken packets or freeze-dried chicken

## Storage Notes

Check package date; shelf-stable packets keep well unopened. Once opened or repackaged, use immediately after opening.

## MiniMo Notes

Drain if needed; add late to avoid overcooking.

## Warnings or Limitations

- Packets are wet and heavier than freeze-dried protein.
- Empty packets can smell; pack out carefully.
