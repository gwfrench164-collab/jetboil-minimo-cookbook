# Dehydrated Vegetables

## Summary

Lightweight vegetable component for soups, ramen, rice, couscous, and pasta.

## Why Carry It

Dehydrated Vegetables is useful because it is shelf-stable, practical for camp cooking, and connects to multiple recipes or meal systems.

## Common Forms

- dried mixed vegetables
- dehydrated peas
- dehydrated carrots
- dried onions
- dried mushrooms

## Best Uses

- backpacking
- soups
- ramen meals
- rice meals
- couscous meals

## Related Recipes

- Beef Jerky Ramen
- Backpacker's Pho
- Just Add Water meal systems

## Related Techniques

- Just Add Water Meals
- Trail Pantry Essentials
- Minimal Ingredient Cooking

## Substitutions

- freeze-dried vegetables
- fresh vegetables
- dried mushrooms

## Fresh vs Shelf-Stable

- Fresh alternative: fresh vegetables
- Shelf-stable alternative: dehydrated vegetables or freeze-dried vegetables

## Storage Notes

Check package date; dry vegetables keep well sealed. Once opened or repackaged, keep dry and sealed; use within a reasonable time after opening.

## MiniMo Notes

Hydrate early; simmer a few minutes before adding quick-cooking bases.

## Warnings or Limitations

- Need hydration time.
- Some vegetables stay chewy if added too late.
