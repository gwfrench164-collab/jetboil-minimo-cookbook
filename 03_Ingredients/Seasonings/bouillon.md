# Bouillon

## Summary

Lightweight broth and flavor base for soups, noodles, rice, and couscous.

## Why Carry It

Bouillon is useful because it is shelf-stable, practical for camp cooking, and connects to multiple recipes or meal systems.

## Common Forms

- bouillon cube
- bouillon powder
- broth concentrate
- stock packet

## Best Uses

- backpacking
- soups
- noodle meals
- rice meals

## Related Recipes

- Backpacker's Pho
- Quick Tortellini Soup
- Beef Jerky Ramen

## Related Techniques

- Just Add Water Meals
- Trail Pantry Essentials
- Minimal Ingredient Cooking

## Substitutions

- broth packet
- stock concentrate
- ramen seasoning

## Fresh vs Shelf-Stable

- Fresh alternative: broth or stock
- Shelf-stable alternative: bouillon cubes, powder, or concentrate packets

## Storage Notes

Check package date; dry cubes/powders keep well sealed. Once opened or repackaged, keep dry and sealed; use promptly if paste/concentrate is opened.

## MiniMo Notes

Start small; you can always add more, but cannot easily remove salt.

## Warnings or Limitations

- Very concentrated and salty.
- A whole cube may be too much for a single serving.
